Bread

Description

Bread is a staple food made from a dough of flour, water, yeast, and salt that is baked or sometimes steamed. It comes in various types, including white, whole wheat, rye, and multigrain, depending on the type of flour used. Bread can be soft and fluffy, with a light, airy texture, or dense and hearty. It is often used as a base for sandwiches, toasted for breakfast, or served alongside soups and stews. Bread can be leavened, using yeast or baking powder to rise, or unleavened, like flatbreads such as tortillas or naan. It is one of the oldest prepared foods, enjoyed across cultures around the world.

  • Flour (all-purpose or bread flour)
  • Water
  • Yeast (active dry or instant)
  • Sugar (optional, to feed the yeast)
  • Salt
  • Olive oil or butter (optional, for richness)

Steps

  1. In a large bowl, mix the flour, salt, and sugar (if using).
  2. In a separate bowl, dissolve the yeast in warm water and let it sit for 5-10 minutes until frothy.
  3. Pour the yeast mixture into the dry ingredients and mix to form a dough.
  4. Knead the dough on a floured surface for 8-10 minutes until smooth and elastic.
  5. Place the dough in an oiled bowl, cover it with a cloth, and let it rise for 1-2 hours until doubled in size.
  6. Punch down the dough and shape it into a loaf or rolls.
  7. Place the dough in a greased loaf pan or on a baking sheet and let it rise again for 30-60 minutes.
  8. Preheat the oven to 375°F (190°C).
  9. Bake the bread for 25-30 minutes or until golden brown and it sounds hollow when tapped.
  10. Let the bread cool on a wire rack before slicing and serving.
